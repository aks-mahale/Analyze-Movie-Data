Introduction¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings,popularity and revenue.It also provides information on cast,directors,genres etc.My goal for this project is to explore interesting trends in the movie data.

Questions To Explore¶

1)What features are associated with popular movies?

2)How budget affects popularity and ratings?

3)What are the most popular movies over the years(1960 to 2015)?

4)What properties are associated with successful movies?

#Function to categorize the quantitative data into quantiles

def categorize_data(column):
    # find quartile, max and min values
    min_value = df[column].min()
    first_quantile = df[column].quantile(0.25)
    second_quantile = df[column].quantile(0.5)
    third_quantile =df[column].quantile(0.75)
    max_value = df[column].max()
    # Bin edges that will be used to "cut" the data into groups
    bin_edges = [ min_value, first_quantile, second_quantile, third_quantile, max_value]
    # Labels for the four budget level groups
    bin_names = [ 'Low', 'Medium', 'Moderately High', 'High'] 
    
# Creates budget_adj_levels column
    new_column='{}_levels'.format(column)
    df[new_column] = pd.cut(df[column], bin_edges, labels=bin_names, include_lowest = True)

Observations¶

  • As we can see, there is a significant correlation between movie budget and revenue, with films with higher production costs generally collecting more revenue.
  • We can see that most high grossing films seem to have been released in the months of June,July,November, and December.
  • The more revenue a films accumulates also seems to correlate with its popularity score and the number of votes it receives.
  • High grossing films also seem to garner higher vote ratings, though this positive correlation is much less pronounced than that between revenue and popularity and vote count.

Conclusions¶

The following relationships were found

  • A positive correlation between movie budget and revenue
  • A positive correlation between revenue and popularity
  • A positive correlation between budget and popularity
  • A positive correlation between vote count and revenue
  • Ratings and runtime do not affect popularity and revenue

Limitations¶

  • Most of the data available are for movies released after 1990.Hence for movies released prior to 1990 relationship between metrices may not be accurate.

  • Since more than half of the data is missing budget and revenue information, final results may not accurately depict trend of successful movies over the years.

  • The budget and revenue columns do not have currency unit.For my analysis I have assumed the currency to be in US dollars.However it might be possible some of the movies in the data have budget in different currency.This may have also impacted the overall analysis.

  • Looking at the correlation between the variables,we may say that they are related but it doesnt imply causation.Further research may be needed investigating whether one action caused the other.
